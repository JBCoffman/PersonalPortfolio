Services & Engagement Types

Work With Me

Senior product leadership — fractional, advisory, or consulting — for companies that can't afford to figure it out by trial and error.

Positioning

Not a contractor. Not a consultant in the generic sense.

The distinction matters. I'm a product executive who works in a fractional or advisory capacity — which means I think like an owner, not a vendor. I'm accountable to outcomes, not deliverables.

Most engagements start with a clear problem: the product org is breaking under growth, the AI investment needs a strategy, or something high-stakes is coming and the current setup won't hold. I come in to fix the root cause, not the symptoms.

The Mangrove Principle

Mangroves are ancient, unglamorous, load-bearing infrastructure. The whole ecosystem collapses without them. Nobody puts them on postcards. That's the work I do — building the system underneath the product that makes everything else possible.

Engagement Types
Engagement 01

Fractional CPO

Embedded, part-time product leadership. I own the roadmap, the team, and the go-to-market alignment — so your organization has executive-level product accountability without a full-time hire.

  • Typically 2–3 days/week over 6–12 months
  • Roadmap strategy and prioritization
  • Team structure, hiring, and development
  • CEO and board communication
  • Go-to-market and revenue alignment
Series B–D No current CPO Scale transition
Engagement 02

Advisory

Structured outside perspective — regular sessions to pressure-test strategy, unblock critical decisions, and keep execution moving. Best for companies with a product leader in place who needs a thinking partner.

  • Typically 2–4 sessions/month
  • Strategy and roadmap review
  • Decision support at key inflection points
  • Investor and board prep
  • On-call access for urgent questions
VP Product in seat High-stakes decision M&A prep
Engagement 03

Consulting

Defined-scope projects around a specific problem. Faster to start, easier to scope, focused on a clear output. Common use cases: AI product strategy, operating model maturity, or team structure design.

  • 4–12 week engagements typical
  • AI monetization and product strategy
  • Product operating model design
  • Platform architecture and roadmap
  • Post-acquisition integration support
Defined problem AI strategy Post-M&A
Problems I Solve

The problems that look like product problems but aren't.

  • Product is shipping but growth isn't following — velocity without direction
  • AI investment approved; no one knows what to build or how to charge for it
  • Acquisition due diligence is exposing gaps in process, governance, or roadmap
  • The team is talented but not aligned — everyone is busy, nothing is moving
  • A new product leadership hire is 6–12 months out and you need coverage now
  • Board and investors are asking strategy questions the current org can't answer
  • You've scaled headcount faster than you've scaled decision-making clarity
  • Revenue retention is eroding; product and customer success are misaligned

Ideal Client Profile

We're probably a fit if…

  • B2B SaaS company between $10M and $200M ARR
  • PE-backed, VC-backed, or NASDAQ-listed
  • Series B, C, or D — a growth inflection is happening or coming
  • Verticals: FinTech, MarTech / SalesTech, or Enterprise IT
  • CEO or founder can engage directly — no 4-layer approval chains
  • You want outcomes, not someone to manage your existing to-do list

We're probably not a fit if…

  • You need a full-time employee with full-time presence on day one
  • The engagement is primarily execution management at the PM level
  • You're pre-product / pre-revenue and need a co-founder
  • The decision-making culture won't support a direct outside voice
  • You want someone to validate a decision already made
What Working Together Looks Like

No onboarding theater.

A typical engagement starts with a fast diagnosis — a structured look at where the real problems are, not just the ones that were mentioned in the first call. Most companies think they have a roadmap problem. The actual problem is usually upstream.

From there, the work is direct. I communicate like an executive, not a consultant — which means I'll tell you what I actually think, what I'd do differently, and what I need from your team to make progress.

40% of my team got promoted in a single year. I take people development seriously. If you have a product team that's underperforming, I'll work with them, not around them.

Week 1–2

Fast diagnosis

Stakeholder conversations, current-state review, problem framing. Output: a shared picture of what's actually going on.

Weeks 3–6

Strategic foundation

Roadmap, operating model, or strategy work — depending on engagement type. Moving from diagnosis to direction.

Ongoing

Embedded execution

Regular cadence, decision support, team development, and course-correction as the business evolves.

FAQ
How much time does a fractional engagement require from me?
Typically 2–4 hours per week from the CEO or key stakeholder — primarily for a weekly sync and async review of key decisions. The goal is to reduce your cognitive load, not add to it. I handle the details.
Do you work with companies that already have a VP Product?
Yes — advisory engagements are specifically designed for companies with product leadership in place. I function as a thinking partner and senior sounding board, not a replacement. The distinction matters, and I'll be clear about it from day one.
What verticals do you know best?
FinTech, MarTech / SalesTech, and Enterprise IT — specifically MSP tooling, intent data, and AI-native B2B platforms. That said, most of what I do is transferable across B2B SaaS. If you're unsure about fit, a 30-minute conversation will answer the question.
How do engagements get priced?
Fractional and advisory engagements are priced monthly, based on scope and time commitment. Consulting projects are typically fixed-fee against a defined scope. Pricing is discussed after an initial conversation — I don't post rates publicly because scope varies significantly.
Are you taking on new clients right now?
Capacity varies. The best way to find out is to reach out. I keep the number of active engagements small by design — I'm not running a consulting firm with staff. When I say I'm available, I'm the one showing up.
What does the initial conversation look like?
30 minutes. You describe what's going on; I ask pointed questions. By the end, we'll both know whether there's a fit and what an engagement might look like. No deck, no sales process, no NDA required for a first call.
Ready to explore a fit?

A 30-minute conversation will tell us both what we need to know.
