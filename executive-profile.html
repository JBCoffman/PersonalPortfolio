Leadership Philosophy

I build the thing under the thing.

Product leadership at the senior level is mostly invisible when it's working. The roadmap is clear. The team is aligned. The org knows how to make decisions without escalating everything. Outcomes are repeatable. That infrastructure doesn't happen accidentally.

I studied Sociology. That background shows up in how I run organizations — I read systems and people together, not separately. Most product problems are people-and-incentives problems wearing a roadmap costume.

I take talent development seriously. 40% of my team getting promoted in a year isn't a distraction from product work — it's the leverage that makes better product work possible.

Pillar 01

Earned confidence

Every claim has receipts. Outcomes, not titles. I lead with what was built and what it produced.

Pillar 02

Direct communication

Short sentences. Active voice. I tell the board, the CEO, and the team what I actually think. It's more efficient.

Pillar 03

Grounded in systems

Sociology background + operator experience. I read people and systems together, not in isolation.
