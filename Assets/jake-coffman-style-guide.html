This is the warmth and wit color. Use it for callout borders, one credential tag, one line of emphasis per section. It earns its impact by not overstaying its welcome. Like a good joke.

03 — Typography

Four fonts, four distinct jobs. Excalifont opens the door — warm, human, disarming. Syne runs the rest of the show — precise, architectural, confident. Instrument Sans handles the conversation. DM Mono handles the metadata. They should never be swapped or mixed outside their defined roles.

04 — Voice & Tone
Pillar 01

Earned

Every claim has receipts. $14M ARR. $1.5B exit. 25 people promoted. The confidence comes from what actually happened, not from self-promotion. Lead with outcomes, follow with story. Never explain why you're good at something — show what it produced.

Pillar 02

Direct

Short sentences. Active voice. "I built." not "I was instrumental in building." Omit throat-clearing — no "I'm passionate about" or "I believe that." The Sociology background means you read people; the writing should feel like you're talking to someone, not broadcasting at them.

Pillar 03

Occasionally Dangerous

One good line per page. Never forced. The BBQ Judge credential sitting next to Scrum Alliance and UC Berkeley is doing more work than any tagline could. "Decent cook." in an executive summary tells you everything about how seriously this person takes themselves — which is: exactly the right amount.

Pillar 04

Grounded

Florida-rooted, not Florida-themed. It shows up in the palette, in "Tampa Bay, FL" in the meta, in the headshot location, in the brand story — not in clipart or clichés. The mangrove metaphor works because it's true, not because it's cute.

The One-Per-Page Rule

Humor earns its place at roughly one instance per page. It must arrive naturally — never set up, never explained, never repeated. The moment you sense you're trying to be funny, you've already missed it. Wit is a byproduct of confidence, not a strategy.

"Decent cook."
✓ Three words. End of executive summary. Perfect delivery. Doesn't explain itself.
"Florida Certified BBQ Judge"
✓ Listed alongside Berkeley Executive Ed and Scrum Alliance. No comment needed.
"40% of my team was promoted in a single year. Some of it was the coaching."
✓ Optional. Dry. True. Earns the laugh by not reaching for it.

06 — Headshot Direction

The headshot should feel like you actually live somewhere real. No studio backdrop, no artificial lighting, no LinkedIn factory energy. Earned, grounded, slightly unexpected.

Location

  • Outdoors — waterfront, mangrove edge, dock, or coastal natural area
  • Manatee County / Tampa Bay geography preferred for authenticity
  • Not a park bench or coffee shop — somewhere that has a story
  • Background slightly out of focus but recognizable as Florida

Light

  • Golden hour (first hour after sunrise or last before sunset) or overcast
  • No harsh midday sun — too flat, washes out your eye color
  • Golden hour warmth complements the Seagrass Gold palette
  • Overcast gives even, dimensional light that reads well at small sizes

Wardrobe

  • Blues work well given your eye color and the overall palette
  • Avoid white — washes against the fog background in web use
  • Avoid busy patterns — competes at small crop sizes
  • Dressed, but not suited — confident, not formal

Expression & Framing

  • Direct eye contact — this is an operator, not a thought leader
  • Slight smile preferred over full smile — matches the dry wit tone
  • Shoot at eye level or slightly below — avoid above-subject angles
  • Get a square crop for social and a landscape version for hero use
